United States of America

The United States of America (USA), commonly known as the United States (U.S.) or simply America, is a country primarily located in North America and consisting of 50 states, a federal district, five major unincorporated territories, and nice minor outlying islands. It includes 326 Indian reservations. It is the world's third-largest country by both Land and total area. It shares land borders with Canada to its north and with Mexico to its south and has maritime borders with the Bahamas, Cuba, Russia, and other nations. With a population of over 333 million, it is the most populous country in the Americas and the third-most populous in the world. The national capital of the United States is Washington, D.C., and its most populous city and principal financial center is New York City.

What is the United States of America's Code?

The United States Code is the codification by subject matter of the general and permanent laws of the United States. It is divided by broad subjects into 53 titles and published by the Office of the Law Revision Counsel of the U.S. House of Representatives. The U.S. Code was first published in 1926. Background History

The United States Code was officially enacted into law as a more modern and comprehensive codification of federal statutes. The U.S. Code was first published in 1926, following the passage of the "Act of June 30, 1926," also known as the "United States Code Law." This law provided for the codification and publication of all existing federal laws. The next main edition was published in 1934, and subsequent main editions have been published every six years since 1934. In between editions, annual cumulative supplements are published in order to present the most current information.
